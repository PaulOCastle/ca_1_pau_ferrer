About the route

Enjoying a journey to the past.

The Limerick Greenway Trail was developed along a stretch of the 85km Limerick to Tralee railway line which operated for nearly one hundred years – opening in the late 1880s and closing in the 1970s. The route, which was also known as the North Kerry line also had branch lines to Foynes in County Limerick and Fenit in County Kerry.

By 1925 the line was owned by the Limerick Greenway which operated all of the railways in the Irish Free State until 1945. After World War II, the GSR was dissolved and the railways came into the ownership of Córas Iompair Éireann (CIÉ). The line stayed in the ownership of CIÉ until the line was closed to passengers in 1963 and freight in the late 1970s.
